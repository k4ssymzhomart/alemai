## TOP LINE

- **Legal/official name is UNCHANGED**: НАО «Фонд социального медицинского страхования» (ФСМС) — "Non-commercial joint-stock company 'Social Medical Insurance Fund'". NOT renamed. CONFIRMED across five independent 2026 sources (see below).
- **What changed**: administrative subordination only. On **16 January 2026** the Government decided to move the Fund from Ministry of Health oversight to **Ministry of Finance** management, following a Minfin audit that found billing irregularities (services billed for deceased patients, cervical-cancer screenings billed for male patients, other приписки/record-padding). CONFIRMED.
- **Current chairman**: **Гульмира Сабденбек (Gulmira Sabdenbek)**, presented to Fund staff on **23 January 2026**. She is a physician by training with an MPH (Master of Public Health) and ~7 years in the Presidential Administration overseeing the social/healthcare sector, reporting on ОСМС matters directly to the President. CONFIRMED, on-the-record in two separate TengriHealth pieces (25 Jan and 12 May 2026).
- **Current primary website**: **msqory.kz** appears to be the current public-facing site (self-titled "ФСМС," content in KK/RU/EN). The legacy domain **fms.kz** is still live and resolving as of today's searches, also self-titled "Фонд социального медицинского страхования," alongside internal subdomains (iss.fms.kz, panel.fms.kz). Both domains currently exist; msqory.kz reads as the newer/primary brand-facing one — LIKELY primary, not 100% confirmed via an explicit migration announcement (see caveats below).
- **Закуп/мониторинг functions in 2026**: Fund CONFIRMED still performs both — it remains the "единый плательщик" (single payer) administering ОСМС contributions AND ГОБМП (state-guaranteed free care budget), and it explicitly continues monitoring/contesting fraudulent billing (see Section 4). No functional split-off found.

## 1. Timeline — CONFIRMED (items 1–3, cross-corroborated)

- **~December 2025**: PM Олжас Бектенов orders Ministry of Finance to audit the Fund's spending effectiveness; experts already hearing rumors of a Minzdrav→Minfin transfer.
- **1 December 2025**: Then-chairman **Айдын Кульсеитов** leaves the post after ~8 months (took office April 2025). Multiple sources note the Fund had 5 different heads in the ~5 years before this exit (per the Jan article) — Sabdenbek's own May interview describes herself as "уже девятый руководитель ФСМС" (the ninth leader overall, a different count base but not contradictory — just counting from the Fund's full history rather than a recent-years window).
- **16 January 2026**: Government formally decides to transfer the Fund to Ministry of Finance management, after Minfin's audit findings were made public (billing for deceased patients, cervical-cancer screenings billed to male patients, systemic приписки). President Tokayev publicly backed the decision, framing it around the "Fair Kazakhstan" strategy.
- **23 January 2026**: Minfin chief-of-staff Эрнар Ержанов presents new leadership to Fund staff: Chairman **Гульмира Сабденбек** and First Deputy Chairman **Айдар Мекебеков** (ex-Minfin, former deputy chair of the Financial Monitoring Committee; immediately prior to this appointment he was director of the anti-fraud department at ТОО "Корпорация Казахмыс").
- **PM's stated goal** (per the Jan article): the Fund's information system was to be integrated into the Ministry of Finance's system "до 1 июля этого года" (by 1 July 2026) — i.e., a deadline that, as of today 2026-07-08, has just passed or is imminent. I found no separate article confirming this integration was actually completed on schedule — flag as a stated plan/target, not a verified completion.
- **16 March 2026**: Pilot launch of the **Qalqan** platform (new digitization/antifraud IT backbone for "ОСМС 2.0").
- **~15 April 2026**: Target deadline (per informburo) for all medical organizations nationwide to be connected to Qalqan; other coverage (pkzsk.info) states 1,700+ clinics and ~5,000 co-contractors connected.
- **12 May 2026**: Chair Sabdenbek gives her first major on-record interview (TengriHealth), explicitly stating the Minfin move is not a mission change, describing ОСМС 2.0/Qalqan as a paradigm shift ("не ребрендинг... смена парадигмы"), and giving budget figures (see Section 4).

## 2. Was the Fund renamed? — CONFIRMED: NO

No source — including the Fund's own chair, on record in May 2026 — describes a legal renaming. The chair's own words: "Передача фонда в Минфин не означает, что ФСМС структурно меняет свою миссию. Он как был организацией, которая обеспечивает доступность здравоохранения для населения, так и остаётся." The "ОСМС 2.0" and "Qalqan" branding refers to a new *operating model and IT platform*, not a new legal entity or name. The legal name confirmed identically across the state procurement registry (goszakup.gov.kz), the Fund's Astana Hub profile, and both fms.kz and msqory.kz self-titles is:

**Некоммерческое акционерное общество «Фонд социального медицинского страхования»** (short form: ФСМС)

## 4. Does it still run закуп (purchasing) and мониторинг (monitoring) in 2026? — CONFIRMED: YES, both

Direct quotes from Chair Sabdenbek, May 2026 interview:

- On monitoring/anti-fraud: "В прошлом году мы в рамках мониторинга, который постоянно проводит ФСМС, оспорили услуги на 38 миллиардов тенге. За первые несколько месяцев этого года — 8 миллиардов тенге." (Last year, through its ongoing monitoring, the Fund contested 38 billion tenge worth of billed services; in the first few months of this year, 8 billion tenge.)
- On its role as single payer: "ФСМС — единый плательщик, он оперирует не только деньгами ОСМС, но и гарантированным объёмом бесплатной медицинской помощи (ГОБМП). В 2026 году речь идёт о сумме 3,4 триллиона тенге: примерно половина — 1,7 триллиона — идёт по линии ГОБМП. Ещё столько же — это ОСМС..." (The Fund is the single payer, handling both ОСМС and ГОБМП. In 2026 that's ~3.4 trillion tenge total: ~1.7 trillion via ГОБМП, ~1.7 trillion via ОСМС.)
- On the new anti-fraud architecture: **Qalqan** platform, pilot from 16 March 2026, is explicitly positioned as the technological backbone for catching fraud *before* payment ("Антифрод... задача антифрода — не разбирать нарушения постфактум, а не дать деньгам уйти из системы изначально") rather than after the fact. Projected (not yet realized) cumulative savings figure cited is 255 billion tenge — the chair is explicit this is a *potential* cumulative effect, not cash already recovered ("это не 'живые' деньги... это потенциальный кумулятивный экономический эффект").

**Strategic implication for IGERIM's pitch (flagging this because it directly affects positioning, not just a fact to report)**: the Fund is already publicly running its own official monitoring/antifraud modernization (ОСМС 2.0 + Qalqan), rolled out March–April 2026, with its own chair actively giving press interviews about it. A pitch that presents OSMS/GOBMP contract-execution monitoring as a greenfield problem risks looking uninformed. Consider explicitly positioning relative to Qalqan (e.g., faster to deploy at clinic/poliklinika level, complementary analytics layer, addresses a gap Qalqan doesn't cover, works during/before full Qalqan rollout, etc.) rather than ignoring it.

## 5. Reorganization — CONFIRMED: administrative only, no functional split found

No evidence of the purchasing or monitoring functions being spun off to a separate body. The only structural change found is the supervising ministry (Minzdrav → Minfin) and the leadership team. The chair explicitly frames the Fund's mission as unchanged. One general caution surfaced in expert commentary (Али Нургожаев, Jan article): historically, when health-adjacent bodies were moved out of Minzdrav before (sanitary-epidemiological service → Ministry of National Economy; Republican e-Health Center → then-Ministry of Digital Development), both were later returned to Minzdrav after "inefficient management" concerns — cited as a cautionary pattern, not a prediction that it will repeat here.
